Terms of Service

General

  • I reserve the right to post commissioned artwork in my socials unless otherwise agreed. I'll add a watermark indicating artwork as commissioned
  • References are necessary for complex commissions and are appreciated for other situations. References can be anything that describes your idea literally or just gives desired vibes: characters, outfits, scenery, screenshots from movies and games, and so on
  • Commissioned artwork is ONLY for personal non-profit usage. If you need it for commercial purposes (merchandise, prints, ads, etc.), we can discuss it, and it will be more expensive too
  • If you plan to share the commissioned artwork publicly, it would be lovely if you credited me, though it's not required
  • I can refuse to work on commission without explanation
  • Everything is still a subject of discussion, and we might agree on a middle ground
  • Everything here could change at any moment without notice. However, I will still try to notify you about any upcoming changes. Also, despite any changes, if we have agreed on something already, it will remain fixed

Alternative versions

  • Alternative versions refer to variations of the same artwork, such as alternative outfit/undressing, hairstyle changes, slight pose adjustments (e.g. small hand/head/eye rotation), minimal background tweaks and so on
  • Not considered as alternative versions: a significantly different pose, a new composition, a new full-body orientation, or any change that requires redrawing major parts of the artwork. These will be treated as a separate commission
  • Revisions vs. alternative versions: Standard revisions only cover minor fixes. Requests that result in an alternative version (as defined above) after final approval will require an extra fee.
  • Pricing: Each alternative version is charged at +15% of the base price
  • Examples:

    Alternative outfit (same pose) → alternative version
    Slight hand/head rotation → alternative version
    Completely new pose or new composition → separate commission

Refunds

  • Refunds are possible, here are the terms:
  • Once the sketch has been created and you made payment, only a partial refund is available
  • The refundable amount will be calculated based on the part of the commission already completed

    For example, if you paid $100 for a illustration and I have completed a knee-up sketch valued at $20, the refund will be $80. If I have completed a knee-up light render illustration valued at 50$, the refund will be $50. And so on

  • After the rendering stage has begun, refunds are no longer possible
  • Be ready if lava.top will eat 8% of payment on refund, I can do nothing about it...
